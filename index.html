Statistical research

Generalities

The research includes the statistical study of a random phenomenon using satistical inference. This inference is based on the establishment of the properties of the phenomenon by studying a part of it. In other words, the study is structured with the propose of drawing conclusions about the population parameter or parameters based on the value of an estimator obtained from the sample data extracted from this population.

Among the pursoses of statistical research we can note:

  • Know the reality about a phenomenon.
  • Determine the normal or typical of a phenomenon.
  • Determine the changes that phenomena present.
  • Relate two or more phenomena.
  • Tabulation of results.
  • Statistical inference.

Components of a statistical investigation

  • Population: It is the set formed by all the possible values that it can assume, the variable object of study.
  • It is any subset of the population chosen by following certain selection criteria.

General procedure

  1. Problem formulation
  2. Experiment design
  3. Data collention
  4. Results tabulation

1 Problem formulation
(Problem statement)

At this point, the question to be answered and the population of data associated with the question must be clearly specified. The concepts must be precise and must place requirements appropiate to the problem that are motivated by the time, money available and the skill of the investigators.

Some concepts such as defective item, economical, salary, may vary in each case and for each problem we must coincide with the ideas indicated in the study.

2 Experiment design
(Modeling)

This aspect is of great importance, since data collection requires money and time, it is always our desire to obtain the maximum information with the minumum cost possible. Including excessive information in the sample is often costly and uneconomical. Including little is also unsatisfactory. This implies, among other things, that we must determine the size of the sample or the amount or type of data that allows us to solve the problem in the most efficient way.

3 Data collection
(Exploration)

This part in general, is the one that requires the most time in the investigation. This collection must comply with scrict rules since we hope to extract to desired information from the data.

4 Results tabulation
(Sequencing)

At this stage, the sample data are clearly exposed and illustrated with tabular and graphical representations (diagrams, histograms, etc.), in addition, the statistical measures appropriate the inferential process that has been chosen are calculated.

5 Statistical inference
(Prediction)

This last step constitutes perhaps the most important contribution of statistics to the inferential process. Here the level of reliability for inference is set; This is because the conclusions derived from statistical inferences can never be taken with 100% certainty, but a level of reliability can be associated with them, in terms of probability called confidence level and significance level.

The inferential process will lead us to a statistical conclusion that will serve as guidance to who or who should make the decision (administrative or clinical) on the subject under study.
